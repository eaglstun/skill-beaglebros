# The Beagle Bros ethos

The voice and the woodcuts are the surface. _This_ is the thing underneath — the part
worth actually importing into how Eric builds. Beagle Bros had a philosophy of how to
treat the people who use your software. It still holds up.

## The 8 principles

1. **Computing should be fun.** Joy is a feature, not a distraction. If using the thing
   doesn't spark a little delight somewhere, you left something on the table.
2. **Demystify, don't gatekeep.** Show people how the machine actually works. Hand over
   the PEEK, the POKE, the trick. Knowledge shared is the whole point.
3. **Trust the user.** They're a collaborator, not a thief or an idiot. Minimal friction,
   minimal nagging, minimal copy-protection-paranoia. Assume competence and good faith.
4. **Documentation is a gift, not a chore.** Make the manual something people _want_ to
   read and _keep_. Useful + delightful, in that order.
5. **Small, sharp, single-purpose.** A tool that does one thing well, with personality,
   beats a bloated suite with none. (DOS Boss did basically one fun thing — brilliantly.)
6. **Generosity as a default.** Free tips, the bonus utility, the "by the way." Give
   more than the transaction requires.
7. **Personality over polish-by-committee.** Software made by _people_ with a voice, not
   sanded down into faceless corporate beige. The maker's hand should show.
8. **Irreverence toward pomposity — never toward the user.** Puncture self-seriousness,
   jargon, and gatekeeping. Protect the person actually trying to get something done.

## "Would Beagle Bros ship this?" — the checklist

Run a thing you're building (a CLI, a README, a feature, a site, an app) through this:

- [ ] **Fun:** Is there a moment of delight in here anywhere, or is it pure utility-beige?
- [ ] **Teaches:** Does it leave the user knowing a little _more_ about how it works, or
      does it keep them dependent and in the dark?
- [ ] **Trusts:** Does it nag, gate, or condescend? Could you remove friction and assume
      competence instead?
- [ ] **Generous:** Does it volunteer one thing the user didn't strictly pay for / ask
      for — a tip, a shortcut, a thoughtful default?
- [ ] **Voiced:** Could you tell a _person_ made this, or is it anonymous corporate
      sludge? Does the maker's hand show?
- [ ] **Honest:** Is every joke riding _alongside_ the truth, never _obscuring_ it?
      Warnings clear, claims real, nothing important buried under a bit?

If most boxes are checked, you've got the sensibility. If it's all utility and no
warmth, or all warmth and no substance, you've got half of it.

## The one-sentence north star

**Treat the user like a smart friend you're delighted to let in on the trick** — and the
voice, the woodcuts, the tips, and the trust all fall out of that automatically.
